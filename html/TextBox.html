Internet protocol

The Internet Protocol (IP) is a fundamental communication protocol that facilitates the transmission of data across networks, including the global Internet. Operating on the principles of packet switching, IP assigns unique numerical labels (IP addresses) to devices, enabling their identification and location. It is a connectionless protocol, breaking down data into packets for efficient transmission, with routers playing a key role in forwarding packets between networks based on destination IP addresses. IPv4 and IPv6 are the two main versions, with IPv6 addressing the limitations of IPv4's finite address space. IP itself does not guarantee reliable delivery, often relying on higher-layer protocols like TCP for that purpose. Overall, the Internet Protocol is crucial for the functioning and connectivity of devices in the digital landscape.
